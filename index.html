Overarching Themes

Ghost figures in the art that followed WWI

About This Exhibit

Haunted Nations explores how artists across the WWI era visualized and processed collective trauma through the figure of the ghost. Whether as luminous specters rising from memorials, intimate companions haunting the living, or stone monuments refusing forgetting, these ghosts embody the inescapable return of the dead.

This digital exhibit brings together six major works spanning Allied and Central Powers perspectives, from Australia to Austria, examining how different artistic traditions grappled with the haunting legacies of the Great War. By mapping thematic connections between artworks, we ask: what does it mean for a nation to be haunted?
